Explore Kerala

Discover the beauty of all 14 districts of Kerala

Top Places to Visit in Kerala:

Munnar

Famous for its tea plantations, rolling hills, and breathtaking views.

Alleppey (Alappuzha)

Known as the "Venice of the East," famous for houseboat cruises and mesmerizing backwaters.

Wayanad

A paradise of lush forests, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries.

Kovalam

Famous for its stunning beaches and vibrant coastal culture.

Thekkady

Home to Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, offering elephant safaris and spice plantations.

Varkala

A scenic coastal town known for its red cliffs and the famous Papanasam Beach.

Thiruvananthapuram

The capital of Kerala, known for its heritage and modern infrastructure. Padmanabhaswamy Temple is one of the wealthiest temples in the world. Kovalam Beach is a famous destination with golden sand and scenic sunsets. The Napier Museum displays Kerala’s rich cultural history. Poovar Island offers breathtaking backwater views. Technopark in the city is one of India’s largest IT hubs. Ponmudi Hill Station provides a refreshing retreat with misty hills and lush greenery.

Must Visit Places:

  • Padmanabhaswamy Temple
  • Kovalam Beach
  • Poovar Island
  • Napier Museum
  • Ponmudi Hill Station

Kollam

Kollam, known as the gateway to the backwaters, is famous for Ashtamudi Lake. The Neendakara Port is crucial to Kerala’s fishing industry. Palaruvi Waterfalls, meaning ‘stream of milk,’ is a mesmerizing attraction. Jatayu Earth’s Center is home to the world’s largest bird sculpture. Kollam Beach is a perfect getaway with a charming lighthouse. The historic Thevally Palace reflects Kerala’s royal past. Munroe Island offers an authentic village backwater experience.

Must Visit Places:

  • Ashtamudi Lake
  • Jatayu Earth’s Center
  • Palaruvi Waterfalls
  • Munroe Island
  • Thevally Palace

Pathanamthitta

Known as the ‘Pilgrim Capital of Kerala,’ it houses the famous Sabarimala Temple. Gavi is a paradise for nature lovers and trekkers. Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls offers a spectacular sight. Konni Elephant Camp is an excellent place to see elephants up close. The Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple is renowned for its unique architecture. The annual Aranmula Boat Race attracts many tourists. The district is known for its metal mirror craft, ‘Aranmula Kannadi.’

Must Visit Places:

  • Sabarimala Temple
  • Gavi Forest
  • Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls
  • Konni Elephant Camp
  • Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple
Book Now

Alappuzha

Known as the Venice of the East, Alappuzha is famous for its backwaters. The houseboat cruises offer a unique Kerala experience. Alappuzha Beach is known for its historic pier. The Nehru Trophy Boat Race is a major attraction. Kuttanad is known as the ‘Rice Bowl of Kerala.’ Marari Beach is perfect for a peaceful retreat. The Mullakkal Rajarajeswari Temple is an important religious site.

Must Visit Places:

  • Alappuzha Backwaters
  • Houseboat Cruises
  • Marari Beach
  • Nehru Trophy Boat Race
  • Mullakkal Temple
Book Now

Ernakulam

The commercial hub of Kerala, home to Marine Drive’s scenic beauty. Fort Kochi is famous for its colonial architecture. The Chinese fishing nets are a symbol of Kochi’s heritage. Jew Town and Mattancherry showcase Kerala’s diverse culture. Lulu Mall is one of India’s largest shopping malls. Cherai Beach offers a mix of backwaters and sea. Ernakulam is also home to the famous Bolgatty Palace.

Must Visit Places:

  • Fort Kochi
  • Chinese Fishing Nets
  • Jew Town
  • Lulu Mall
  • Cherai Beach
Book Now

Kottayam

Kottayam is known for its literacy rate and publishing industry. The beautiful Vembanad Lake is a major attraction. Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary is home to migratory birds. It is a hub for rubber plantations in Kerala. The ancient Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple is located here. Ilaveezhapoonchira is a scenic trekking spot. The district is also famous for its backwaters and spice plantations.

Must Visit Places:

  • Kumarakom Backwaters
  • Vembanad Lake
  • Illikkal Kallu
  • Bharananganam Church
  • Vaikom Mahadeva Temple
Book Now

Idukki

Idukki is famous for its breathtaking tea plantations in Munnar. The Idukki Dam is one of Asia’s largest arch dams. Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary is home to elephants and tigers. The district is covered with lush forests and hills. Vagamon offers beautiful meadows and pine forests. Ramakkalmedu provides stunning views of Tamil Nadu. Thekkady is a famous tourist destination with spice plantations.

Must Visit Places:

  • Munnar
  • Thekkady
  • Idukki Arch Dam
  • Kalvari Mount
  • Ramakkalmedu
Book Now

Thrissur

Known as the Cultural Capital of Kerala, Thrissur hosts the famous Thrissur Pooram festival. Vadakkunnathan Temple is an iconic landmark. The Kerala Kalamandalam promotes classical arts. Athirappilly Waterfalls is often called the ‘Niagara of India.’ Guruvayur Temple attracts thousands of devotees. Punnathur Kotta houses temple elephants. Thrissur is also famous for its gold jewelry industry.

Must Visit Places:

  • Vadakkunnathan Temple
  • Athirapally Waterfalls
  • Guruvayur Temple
  • Punnathur Kotta Elephant Sanctuary
  • Kerala Kalamandalam
Book Now

Palakkad

Palakkad is known as the ‘Granary of Kerala’ due to its vast paddy fields. The historic Palakkad Fort is a major landmark. Silent Valley National Park is home to rare wildlife. Malampuzha Dam is a scenic picnic spot. The district has beautiful mountain ranges and rivers. Nelliyampathy is a popular hill station. Kalpathy Ratholsavam is a famous temple festival celebrated here.

Must Visit Places:

  • Palakkad Fort
  • Silent Valley National Park
  • Malampuzha Dam
  • Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Dhoni Waterfalls
Book Now

Malappuram

Malappuram has a rich history and cultural heritage. Kottakkunnu is a beautiful hill garden. Nilambur Teak Museum is the first of its kind in India. The district is famous for its football culture. Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary is home to numerous migratory birds. Ponnani Beach is a scenic coastal destination. Malappuram also has several ancient mosques and temples.

Must Visit Places:

  • Kodikuthimala
  • Kottakkunnu
  • Nedumkayam Rainforest
  • Pazhayangadi Mosque
  • Teak Museum
Book Now

Kozhikode

Kozhikode is where Vasco da Gama first landed in India. The district is famous for its delicious Malabar cuisine. Kozhikode Beach is a popular tourist attraction. Beypore is known for its ancient shipbuilding industry. Kappad Beach has historical significance. The city is a major educational hub. The famous Mananchira Square is a cultural hotspot.

Must Visit Places:

  • Kozhikode Beach
  • Beypore Port
  • Thusharagiri Waterfalls
  • Kappad Beach
  • Mananchira Square
Book Now

Wayanad

Wayanad is known for its picturesque landscapes and wildlife. Edakkal Caves have ancient petroglyphs. Chembra Peak has a heart-shaped lake. Banasura Sagar Dam is the largest earthen dam in India. Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is home to tigers and elephants. Soochipara Waterfalls is a stunning trekking spot. The district has many spice and coffee plantations.

Must Visit Places:

  • Edakkal Caves
  • Chembra Peak
  • Soochipara Waterfalls
  • Banasura Sagar Dam
  • Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary
Book Now

Kannur

Kannur is famous for its Theyyam performances. Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach is the longest in Asia. St. Angelo Fort is a historic Portuguese fort. Arakkal Museum showcases Kerala’s only Muslim royal family. Payyambalam Beach is a serene getaway. The district is known for its handloom industry. Kannur is also an important center for folklore and traditions.

Must Visit Places:

  • Payyambalam Beach
  • Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach
  • St. Angelo Fort
  • Parassinikadavu Temple
  • Arakkal Museum
Book Now

Kasaragod

Kasaragod is famous for Bekal Fort, Kerala’s largest fort. The district has beautiful backwaters and beaches. Ananthapura Lake Temple is a unique attraction. The Chandragiri Fort offers stunning river views. Kumbala is known for its annual buffalo race. The district has a mix of Malayalam, Tulu, and Kannada cultures. Ranipuram is a popular trekking and nature destination.

Must Visit Places:

  • Bekal Fort
  • Chandragiri Fort
  • Ananthapura Lake Temple
  • Ranipuram Hills
  • Valiyaparamba Backwaters
